# Basic mathematical concepts

These are the mathematical concepts used in the documentation. It is far from complete.

***
<a id="mathematical-object"></a>
- **object**: a unique thing

***
<a id="pair"></a>
- **pair** :  $(x,y)\mapsto (x,y)$
  - note: ordered pair of two objects

***

<a id="mathematical-set"></a>
- **set** : $(x_1, x_2, \dots)\mapsto\{x_1, x_2, \dots\}$
  - note: collection of distinct objects
***

- **Bool** = $\{$ *true*, *false*$\}$
***

- **predicate** : $X \to $ Bool
  - note: a condition for a selection
***

- **selection**: $(X, p) \mapsto \{ x\in X \mid p(x)=$ *true* $\}$

***

- **element relation**: $(x, X) \mapsto (x \in X) \in $ Bool
  - $x \in X = $ *true* if $x$ is an element of $X$
  - $x \in X = $ *false* otherwise

***

- **cardinality**:  $X \mapsto |X| \in \mathbb{N} \cup \{\infty\}$
  - note: number of elements in a list

***

- **subset relation**: $Y$, $X \mapsto (Y \subseteq X) \in $ Bool
    - $ Y \subseteq X = $ *true* if every element of $Y$ is an element of $X$
    - $ Y \subseteq X = $ *false* otherwise

***

- **superset relation**: $Y$, $X \mapsto (Y \supseteq X) \in$ Bool
    - $ Y \supseteq X = $ *true* if every element of $X$ is an element of $Y$
    - $ Y \supseteq X = $ *false* otherwise

***

- **power set**: $X \mapsto  \mathcal{P}(X) = \{ Y \mid Y\subseteq X\}$
  - note: a set whose elements are subsets of a set

***

<a id="list"></a>
- **list**: ↩  [set](#mathematical-set) = $(x_1, x_2, \dots)$
  
***

- **sublist**: list $L \to$ list obtained by removing elements from $L$ while preserving order
  - note: oreder set

***
<a id="successor"></a>
- **successor**: $L\mapsto x\in L \mapsto x'\in L$
  - note: gives the successor element

***
<a id="natural-number"></a>
- **natural number**  $\to\mathbb{N} = (1,2,\dots)$
    - [successor](#successor)$(\mathbb{N}): n \mapsto n+1$

***
<a id="cartesian-product"></a>
- **Cartesian product**:  $(X, Y) \to X \times Y = \{ (x,y) \mid x \in X,\; y \in Y \}$
    - note:
      the set of all ordered pairs with first element from $X$ and second from $Y$

***

<a id="map"></a>
- **map** : $(X, Y) \mapsto M \subseteq X \times Y$
  - notes:
    - a map may associate zero, one, or multiple elements of $Y$ to an element of $X$
    - relation
***

- **domain**: $\operatorname{Dom} : M \mapsto \{ x \in X \mid \exists y \in Y:\; (x,y) \in M \}$
  - note: the possible arguments of a map
***

- **range**: $\operatorname{Ran}: M \to \{ y \in Y \mid \exists x \in X:\; (x,y) \in M \}$
  - note: the possible values of a map

***
<a id="image"></a>
- **image** : $\mathcal{M}: (M \subseteq X \times Y$, $A \subseteq X) \mapsto \{\, y \in Y \mid \exists x \in A:\; (x,y) \in M \,\}$
    - special case ($A=\{x\}$):
        - image of $x$
        - fiber over $x$
    - notes:
      - fiber of a map
      - generalizes the image of a single element

***
<a id="inverse-image"></a>
- **inverse image** : $\mathcal{M}^{-1}: M \subseteq X \times Y$, $B \subseteq Y\to \{\, x \in X \mid \exists y \in B:\; (x,y) \in M \,\}$
    - notes:
      - the set of all elements of $X$ that are related to at least one element of $B$
      - preimage

***
<a id="total-map"></a>
- **total map**: ↩ [map](#map), where $\operatorname{Dom}(M) = X$

***
<a id="function"></a>
- **function**:  ↩ [map](#map): $f:X\to Y$, where $\forall x \in X:\; |\mathcal{M}(x)| = 1$
    
***
<a id="partition-of-a-set"></a>
- **partition**: $C \subset \mathcal{P}(X)$
  - conditions:
    - completeness condition:
      $\displaystyle \bigcup_{c \in C} c = X$
    - disjointness condition:
      $c \cap c' = \emptyset
      \quad \text{for } c \neq c',\; c,c' \in C$
  - interpretation:
      each element of $X$ belongs to exactly one element of $C$
  - induced structure:
      σ-algebra generated by the partition

***
<a id="sigma-algebra"></a>
- **σ-algebra**: $(X, C\subseteq \mathcal{P}(X)) \mapsto \sigma(C) \subseteq \mathcal{P}(X)$
  - conditions:
    - $\forall c\in C:\; c\in\sigma(C)$
    - $\emptyset \in \sigma(C)$
    - $X \in \sigma(C)$
    - closed under complement
    - closed under countable unions
  - construction of the smallest σ-algebra:  
      $\displaystyle
      \sigma(C)
      = \left\{
          \bigcup_{i \in J} c_i
          \;\middle|\;
          J \subseteq I
        \right\},
      \quad C=\{c_i\}_{i\in I}$

***
<a id="measurable-space"></a>
- **measurable space** = $(X, \sigma(\mathcal{P}(X)))$
